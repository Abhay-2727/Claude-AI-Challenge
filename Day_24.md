📚 Today's Biggest Learning

A great startup isn't built by adding more features—it's built by validating stronger assumptions.

While creating the Business Strategy Report, I learned that investors don't evaluate startups the way founders do. 
Founders often focus on what the product does, but investors focus on who pays, why they'll pay, how you'll acquire customers, 
and whether the business can scale profitably. The biggest shift in my thinking was realizing that distribution and revenue matter 
more than feature lists. A startup with a simple product and a clear path to customers is often stronger than a feature-rich product with no 
proven market demand.
