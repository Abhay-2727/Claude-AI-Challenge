**What i learned:**
Prompt Engineering is the practice of designing, refining, and optimizing the instructions you give to an AI model to get the most accurate, useful, and relevant outputs. It's essentially the art of "talking to AI effectively.

**Why does it matter?**
1. Same model, wildly different results. Two people using the same AI can get completely different quality of answers — just based on how they phrase their request. Prompt engineering is what separates a frustrating experience from a powerful one.
2. It multiplies productivity. A well-engineered prompt can compress hours of work into seconds. Whether you're coding, writing, analyzing data, or brainstorming — the better your prompt, the faster and more accurate the result.
3. It's a universal skill. Every AI tool — whether it's Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Midjourney, or Copilot — responds to better inputs with better outputs. Learning prompt engineering works across all of them.
4. It reduces errors and hallucinations. Clear, constrained prompts give the model less room to go off-track. Adding context ("assume the audience is a non-technical manager") or format instructions ("respond in bullet points") anchors the output.
